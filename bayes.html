Bayes' Theorem

Let’s break it down from scratch.

Suppose we know \( P(B \mid A) \), and we want to know \( P(A \mid B) \). That’s where Bayes steps in:

$$ P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(B \mid A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} $$

We'll explore why this works — and try a few real-world examples to stumble our way into the formula naturally.
