Bayes' Theorem

Let’s build this one from curiosity.

We want to know \( P(A \mid B) \), but we might know \( P(B \mid A) \). That’s where Bayes steps in:

$$ P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(B \mid A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} $$

It’s not black magic — it’s just the definition of conditional probability rearranged. More on that soon.
