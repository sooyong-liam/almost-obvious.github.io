Bayes' Theorem

Flipping the conditional like a logical pancake.

The Setup

We want to compute \( P(A \mid B) \), but we only know \( P(B \mid A) \). That’s where Bayes jumps in:

$$ P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(B \mid A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} $$

Worked Example

A test is 99% accurate... what’s the chance you actually have the disease if you test positive?

Classic Bayes problem — let’s work it through:

$$ P(D \mid +) = \frac{P(+ \mid D) \cdot P(D)}{P(+)} $$
